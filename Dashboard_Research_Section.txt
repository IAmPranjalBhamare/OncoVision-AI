Section 5. Integrated Clinical Dashboard and Implementation

To bridge the gap between deep learning theory and clinical practice, OncoVision AI is deployed as a high-fidelity web-based diagnostic portal. This system is engineered using a Flask-based micro-architecture, ensuring real-time inferencing with minimal latency.

5.1. Real-time Diagnostic Pipeline
Upon ingestion of a raw ultrasound scan, the system executes a sequential diagnostic pipeline:
1. Signal Restoration: Application of BM3D filters to suppress speckle noise without losing structural margins.
2. Triple-View Generation: Simultaneously executing the U-Net (Segmentation) and EDCNN (Classification) models.
3. XAI Synthesis: Generating SmoothGrad-CAM++ heatmaps to justify the model's prediction.

[FIGURE 5: OncoVision AI Interactive Dashboard - Placement: Middle of page after Section 5.1]

5.2. Clinical Narrative Engine & XAI Metrics
The dashboard interprets quantitative AI scores into radiological insights. It calculates three internal XAI metrics:
- Target Alignment (%): Measures the spatial correlation between the segmentation mask and the model's focus.
- Tissue Coverage (%): Quantifies the relative volume of the suspicious lesion.
- Signal Concentration: A certainty index derived from the peak activation of the fusion map.
Based on these, the system generates a "Clinical Narrative"—a text summary that explains the AI's logic to the attending physician.

5.3. Automated Reporting & In-Memory Security
For documentation, the system generates a multi-pane clinical PDF report including diagnostic fusions, category probabilities, and urgency indicators. To ensure HIPAA-level patient privacy, the platform utilizes a "Zero-Disk" policy where all image data and processed heatmaps are stored exclusively in volatile memory (RAM) and permanently wiped upon session termination.
